Malko Turnovo

Malko Tarnovo is located 76 km south of Bourgas, 56 km southwest of Tsarevo and 7 km from the border with the Republic of Turkey. It is located in the heart of the Bulgarian Strandzha, in a valley surrounded by high hills between the rivers Rezovska and Mechi dol. The surroundings of the city offer a wide choice of interesting places for recreation in nature - preserved wooded areas with numerous springs, chapels, over 60 centuries old trees. Only 5 km southwest is the highest point in the Bulgarian Strandzha - Golyamo Gradishte peak (710 m), from where there is a spacious view of the hills of Strandzha, and in clear weather you can see the Black Sea.

The big scream

The name of the town is also connected with the Great Heath - according to some local historians it originates from "thorn" (Trunovo - Tarnovo) because of the thick thorns, among which were hidden the sacred spring and its guardians - the fairies. According to others, it comes from Terra nova (terra/land/- nova/water) - Terranova - Ternova - Tarnovo - "land by the water". In fact, the two hypotheses are close and seem to complement each other. The green heart of Strandzha - Malko Tarnovo - is pounding, and it can't be otherwise, because it draws energy from the magical power of its spring - the Great Heath.





Thracian cult complex in Mishkova Niva

It is located on the southeastern slope of Golyamo Gradishte Peak (710 m above sea level), about 3 km southwest of M. Tarnovo. The complex includes a domed tomb-sanctuary, the remains of a fortified building (Villa Rustika - the residence of the servants of the temple), a mound necropolis, an ancient aqueduct, a mine and the ruins of a fortress located on the very top. The cult building was also the tomb of a Thracian tribal chief priest and hero. In the V-III century BC.

Thracian necropolis and tombs in the area of Propada

It is located 3 km northwest of Malko Tarnovo, on the slope of a low hill with about 40 burial mounds. It is a complex of tombs from different eras and architectural solutions: a dome tomb from the IV - III century BC, a tomb with a base carved into natural rock, a gabled tomb and cyst graves (burial chambers built of large stone slabs). They all follow an old building tradition and illustrate the transition from a dolmen to a tomb.
