Tsarevo


Peshtera Maharata

The cave itself is located very close to Rezovska river. It has sunk into a deep ravine, and a small river descends over its entrance, which disappears into its bowels. The Maharata is an ancient Thracian sanctuary of the mother goddess. It is built of many underground galleries. Today, however, they are covered with stones and can only be reached by the main gallery of the cave. It is said that the tunnels reach as far as the Rezovska River, but this cannot be proven. It is possible that in the past the ancient Thracians even mined ore here.

Kondolovo

Kondolovo, located in the Strandja Nature Park, is a small village, popular mostly for its large locality of the rare plant from prehistoric times - Strandzha periwinkle. In its vicinity there is a centuries-old deciduous forest, which is a real treasure for other interesting and rare plants. Apart from its natural resources, Kondolovo is interesting for tourists because of the preserved old Strandzha houses from the middle of the 19th century.Today the village is almost depopulated, the population is only 16 people. It is 24 km west of Tsarevo and 33 km east of Malko Tarnovo.

Kosti

The village of Kosti, part of the Strandzha Nature Park, is popular for its firefighting customs, the deciduous centuries-old forests in its vicinity, the preserved old houses, as well as its church, which is one of the most beautiful village churches in the region. Its proximity to the Black Sea resort of Tsarevo located 24 km.

Varvara

According to the local legend about the origin of the name of the village in antiquity, the place of today's Varvara was inhabited by Thracian tribes, which are engaged in special agriculture and animal husbandry. They often attacked the neighboring city of Agatopolis (today's Ahtopol). It was inhabited by Greeks, who began to call them barbarians, and hence the name of their village - Barbara.
